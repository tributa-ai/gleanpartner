Evaluation and recommended complements

The original plan is economically attractive, but these additions make it more investor-ready and operationally safer for Brazil execution.

What is already strong

  • Dual engine of resale margin plus high-margin services.
  • Deal mix intentionally migrates from co-sell to partner-originated revenue.
  • Working-capital gap is short enough to be financeable.
  • Commercial headcount ramp is disciplined versus target volume.

What is missing from the plan

  • G&A, taxes, legal, and entity setup costs for Brazil are not yet layered in.
  • No delivery-capacity model validates the 40% services margin at scale.
  • No churn, renewal, or expansion assumptions support long-term channel value.
  • No FX protection or credit policy is defined despite a Brazil operating context.

Best complements to add

  • Add quarterly pipeline-coverage targets by motion A, B, and C.
  • Add a services utilization plan by role and expected delivery mix.
  • Add a credit-line policy sized to the bridge shown in this model plus contingency.
  • Add enablement milestones, reference wins, and renewal-governance checkpoints.
